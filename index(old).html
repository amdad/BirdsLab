BIRDS Lab

We are a team of University of Michigan undergraduates, graduate students, and professors creating robotic movement systems that emulate biology.

Our Mission

Modular Design

By using modular robotic parts, it becomes inexpensive to build adaptive machines.

Biomechanics

When an animal loses a leg, it eventually adapts. It is possible to play frisbee with a three-legged dog. Robotics does not measure up to biology in this respect, and overcoming this is part of what BIRDS Lab is for.

Teaching

BIRDS Lab is associated with various educational programs around the University of Michigan, including EECS 498, EECS 562, and ME 450.

Research

BIRDS Lab is breaking new ground in bio-robotics research. We publish!
